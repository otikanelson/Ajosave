**AJOSAVE GOALS - OVERALL VISION FOR AJOSAVE**

**The Problem We're Solving:**

AjoSave addresses a critical gap in Nigeria's financial ecosystem where over 60% of adults remain unbanked or underbanked, yet millions participate in traditional rotating savings groups known as "Ajo," "Esusu," or "Adashi." These community-based savings systems move an estimated ₦500 billion annually but operate entirely through manual processes, exposing participants to fraud, mismanagement, lack of transparency, and zero access to formal credit despite consistent savings behavior. When treasurers disappear with funds or disputes arise over contributions, trust breaks down, and entire savings groups collapse, leaving members financially vulnerable with no recourse. AjoSave was created to preserve the cultural and social value of traditional cooperative savings while eliminating these critical flaws through technology, bringing transparency, security, automation, and financial inclusion to millions of Nigerians who have been left behind by traditional banking systems.

## ****Our Solution & Vision****

AjoSave is our answer to a problem we've seen affect real people in our communities. We're building a platform where anyone can create or join savings groups digitally, contribute money safely, track everything that happens with their funds, and eventually access loans based on how consistently they've been saving. It's simple: take the traditional Ajo system that has worked for generations and make it work better by removing the parts that cause problems-like money disappearing, unclear records, and broken trust.

**What We're Really Trying to Do:** Make it possible for everyday Nigerians-the market woman, the Uber driver, the student, the barber-to save money together without worrying about fraud, and to actually build a financial track record that can help them get credit when they need it. Right now, banks don't see these people. We do.

**Where We're Headed:** We want AjoSave to become the go-to platform for group savings in Nigeria first, then across West Africa. Anywhere people practice rotating savings-whether it's called Ajo, Esusu, Susu, or Stokvel-we believe our platform can help.

### ****Roadmap:****

**First 6 Months - Startup Era**

- Launch the mobile app and finish the web platform properly
- Get about 1,000 real users to try it out-people from Lagos, Ibadan, Kano
- Partner with maybe 10 market associations who already run Ajo groups
- Help people save at least ₦50 million total (sounds like a lot but it's really just 1,000 people each saving ₦50k)
- Make sure people actually stick around and don't abandon the app after one use
- Get our legal docs sorted-CBN compliance, all the regulatory paperwork
- Figure out how to score people's creditworthiness based on their savings patterns

**Next Year or So - Growth Era**

- Grow to around 50,000 users across 10 states in Nigeria
- Help people save about ₦2 billion collectively
- Start lending-maybe ₦500 million in loans to about 5,000 people who've proven they save consistently
- Partner with real microfinance banks who can help us with bigger credit facilities
- Work with 100 different cooperatives, market associations, church groups, whatever
- Actually make money-like ₦5 million monthly from small fees and loan interest
- Add better features: scheduled savings, maybe insurance, investment options
- Set up agents in major cities so people can deposit or withdraw cash if they need to

**Year 2-3 - Scale Across Nigeria**

- Reach 500,000 users everywhere in Nigeria, not just big cities
- Facilitate about ₦20 billion in savings annually
- Give out ₦5 billion in loans to 50,000 people
- Actually become profitable-making more than ₦50 million monthly
- Build a version for bigger groups: cooperatives, unions, small business associations
- Work with multiple payment providers so people have options
- Partner with at least 10 banks or financial institutions
- Open support centers in Lagos, Abuja, PH, Kano, Enugu
- Get proper licenses: digital lending license, maybe even microfinance banking license

**Year 4-5 - Go Beyond Nigeria(Global Era)**

- Expand to Ghana, Kenya, and South Africa where similar savings cultures exist
- Hit 10 million users across multiple African countries
- Move ₦100 billion+ in savings every year
- Help 2 million people access credit who couldn't get it anywhere else
- Become a recognizable brand for cooperative savings in Africa
- Add more financial products: insurance, investments, remittances for people sending money home
- Partner with big pan-African banks and development organizations
- Create real jobs-maybe 5,000 people working directly or indirectly with us

### ****What Success Actually Looks Like:****

Beyond the numbers, here's what we really want to achieve:

We want to see a million Nigerians who've never had bank accounts suddenly have digital financial identities that mean something. We want market women (who make up most Ajo groups anyway) to have the same access to credit that salaried workers take for granted. We want to basically eliminate the "my Ajo admin ran away with our money" stories that happen too often. We want half a million people to get their first real loan without needing collateral or a salary slip-just proof that they save consistently.

Most importantly, we want to show that you don't have to abandon African financial traditions to go digital. Ajo worked for our parents and grandparents because it was built on community and trust. We're not replacing that-we're just making it safer, clearer, and more powerful. We're proving that the millions of Nigerians the banks call "unbankable" are actually great savers and responsible borrowers who just need the right tools.

Honestly? We're building this because we're tired of seeing people work hard, save diligently, and still get screwed over by broken systems. We think we can fix that. And if we do it right, we won't just build a successful company-we'll actually change lives and prove that African fintech can be built on African solutions, not just copies of Silicon Valley apps.

That's the vision. That's what gets us excited to build this thing.
